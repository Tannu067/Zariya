Her Voice

1. I Used to Cry in the Shower

"When my son was diagnosed, I felt like the ground was pulled from beneath me. I didn't know what autism meant. I Googled it endlessly, each time feeling more lost. I used to cry in the shower, so no one would hear. I blamed myself, questioned everything I did during pregnancy. But over time, I realized — he didn't need fixing, he needed understanding. The world may not always be kind, but I will always be his safe space."
